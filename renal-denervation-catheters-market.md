<p><p>Renal denervation catheters are medical devices used in a minimally invasive procedure aimed at treating hypertension by disrupting the renal sympathetic nerves. This novel approach is particularly significant for patients who do not respond to conventional antihypertensive medications. The catheters deliver targeted radiofrequency or ultrasound energy to the renal arteries, leading to a reduction in nerve activity and, subsequently, blood pressure.</p><p>The market for renal denervation catheters is experiencing noteworthy growth, driven by an increasing prevalence of hypertension and related cardiovascular diseases. <p><p>Renal denervation catheters are categorized into two main types: disposable and non-disposable. Disposable catheters are designed for single-use, ensuring sterility and reducing the risk of infection, making them preferred for outpatient procedures. Non-disposable catheters, on the other hand, are intended for multiple uses after proper sterilization, often resulting in lower long-term costs but requiring stringent maintenance protocols. The choice between these types depends on factors such as clinical setting, cost considerations, and patient safety.</p></p>
