<p><p>The industrial central vacuum cleaner market is categorized into two main types: centralized and distributed. Centralized types feature a single, powerful vacuum unit that serves multiple endpoints through a network of pipes, ideal for large facilities needing extensive suction capability. In contrast, distributed types comprise several smaller vacuum units installed at various locations, allowing for more flexible and localized cleaning solutions, suitable for smaller spaces or specific areas with unique requirements. Both systems enhance efficiency and maintain cleanliness in industrial settings.</p></p>
