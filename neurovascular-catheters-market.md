<p><p>The neurovascular catheters market comprises several types, each tailored for specific interventional procedures. Microcatheters are ultra-thin tubes used for precise access to small blood vessels. Balloon catheters are designed to inflate and open narrowed vessels during procedures like angioplasty. Access catheters facilitate the entry of instruments into the vascular system. Embolization catheters are used to deliver agents that block blood flow for treatment of conditions like tumors. Other catheters might include diagnostic and therapeutic devices, each contributing to advancing neurovascular interventions.</p></p>
